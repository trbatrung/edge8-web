1. What we heard

Rentwest runs about 2,200 rental properties across Perth with roughly 40 staff and a small sales team, twenty years in this year. The constraint is not effort or talent. Property management is a deeply siloed software industry, and the tools do not share one source of truth.

  • Too many systems. At one point about 15 separate tools plus the Microsoft suite, each solving one slice of the problem, each its own login and MFA.
  • No single view. Your core records sit in one system, inspections in another, applications in a third, files in Teams and SharePoint, sales in a legacy Airtable exported to a spreadsheet. Nowhere shows an owner, their property, and everything attached to them at once.
  • The cost is time. A straight answer means hopping between systems. The real goal, the reason you ran the four-day-week pilot, is to give that time back to the team.
  • The groundwork is done. With Andreas you have already built the AI vision and guardrails, the role scorecards, and the company-knowledge capture. That is exactly the input this build needs.

2. The core insight

This is not an AI problem. The speed of AI is exposing a data problem. Everything you want rests on two things: one central database, and clearly defined workflows on top of it. Get those right and the quick wins stop being clever one-offs and become how the business runs. Get them wrong and no tool, however good, can give you a clean answer fast.

A vector database is one ingredient. The foundation is simpler and more durable: a clean, relational company database that you own, organised around how your business actually works, with the scattered apps either connected to it or retired into it.

3. The build: one company operating system

We model every company as four functions, which we call offices, and we structure the database to match. For a property management business it maps cleanly:

Revenue

Owners, properties & leases

Owners, the properties they hold, leases, rent, and the sales pipeline. The holistic owner and property view, in one place.

Operations

Inspections, maintenance & applications

Routine inspections, maintenance jobs, and tenant applications. The work that today sits in three or four separate apps.

Talent

Team & scorecards

Your 40 staff, the role scorecards you have already built, capacity, and workload, tied to the work they actually do.

Innovation

New ideas & improvement

Where new initiatives, automations, and experiments are tracked, so improvement compounds instead of getting lost.

The stack underneath is the same one we run our own companies on, and the same one we use on every client engagement. It is Claude-native, which suits the fact that Claude is already your team’s favourite:

Rentwest owns / controls Apps to database Migration in Your system of record

Nothing here is exotic. It is a standard, modern stack: Claude as the brain, GitHub for the code, Supabase for the database, Vercel to put it on the web, sized to Rentwest. As the data moves in, you notice apps you no longer need, and you build the replacement straight on top of your own database. We run our own companies with zero third-party apps this way.

4. How we’d phase it

Three phases, each a standalone win. You set the pace: incremental, one component at a time, or a bigger push when it suits you.

1
The foundation

One database & the holistic owner view

We define the first problem worth solving precisely, find where every piece of data lives, and design a clean company data model. Then we stand up your central database and bring the first data in, so an owner, their properties, leases, inspections, and applications finally sit in one place. This is the hardest and most valuable step. On its own it removes the go-through-five-systems tax.

2
The rebuild

Reports & workflows on your own data

With clean data in place, we rebuild the work your team does by hand: inspection scheduling, tenant applications, owner reporting, running straight off your own database. We replace one workflow at a time, in the order that gives the most relief first, so nothing breaks and you stay in control.

3
The replacement

Retire the apps you no longer need

Once a workflow runs on your own system and is proven, we switch off the app it replaced and its licence comes off your books. What remains is a small hosting cost and a system that is yours, tailored exactly how you want it.
