1. What we heard

Rentwest runs ~2,200 rental properties across the Perth metro with about 40 staff and a small sales team, twenty years in this year. The constraint is not effort or talent — it is that property management is a deeply siloed software industry, and the tools do not share one source of truth.

  • Too many systems. At one point ~15 separate tools (plus the Microsoft suite), each solving one slice of the problem, each its own login and MFA.
  • No holistic view. Your main operating system holds the core records, but inspections live in one app, tenant applications in another, files in Teams and SharePoint, sales in a legacy Airtable exported to a spreadsheet. There is no single place to see an owner, their property, and everything attached to them.
  • The cost is time. Getting a straight answer means going through several systems. Your real goal — the reason you ran the four-day-week pilot — is to give that time back to the team.
  • The groundwork is already laid. With Andreas you have done the AI vision and guardrails work, the role scorecards, and the company-knowledge capture. That is exactly the input a build like this needs.

2. The core insight

This is not an AI problem — the speed of AI is exposing a data problem. Everything you want rests on two things: one central database, and clearly defined workflows on top of it. Get those right and the “quick wins” stop being clever one-offs and become how the business runs. Get them wrong and no tool, however good, can give you a clean answer fast.

A “vector database” is one ingredient, but the foundation is simpler and more durable than that: a clean, relational company database that you own, organised around how your business actually works, with the scattered apps either connected to it or retired into it.

We model every company as four functions — we call them offices — and the database is structured to match. For a property management business it maps cleanly:

Inspections, maintenance & applications

Team & scorecards

Your 40 staff, the role scorecards you have already built, capacity, and workload — tied to the work they actually do.

Innovation

New ideas & improvement

Where new initiatives, automations, and experiments are tracked — so improvement compounds instead of getting lost.

4. How we’d phase it

Three phases, each a standalone win. You set the pace — incremental, one component at a time, or a bigger push when it suits you.

1
The foundation

One database & the holistic owner view

We define the first problem worth solving precisely, discover where every piece of data lives, and design a clean company data model. Then we stand up your central database and bring the first data in — so an owner, their properties, leases, inspections, and applications finally sit in one place. This is the hardest and most valuable step; on its own it removes the “go through five systems” tax.

2
The rebuild

Reports & workflows on top of your data

With clean data in place, we rebuild the work your team does by hand — inspection scheduling, tenant applications, owner reporting — running straight off your own database. We replace one workflow at a time, in the order that gives the most relief first, so nothing breaks and you stay in control.

3
The replacement

Retire the apps you no longer need

Once a workflow runs on your own system and is proven, we decommission the app it replaced and its licence comes off your books. What remains is a small hosting cost and a system that is yours, tailored exactly how you want it.
