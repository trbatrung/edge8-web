What we heard

Your current setup

You run a 50-person commercial plumbing business on a stack of separate tools that don't share one source of truth:

  • Simpro for operations: scheduling, labour and material cost capture, job budgets, and invoicing (plus the new "Lightning" AI add-on you're trialling).
  • Xero for financials, overheads, and wages, fed from Simpro.
  • Easy Bills to read supplier invoices from your accounts inbox, which still misreads enough that a part-time staffer matches them to jobs by hand.
  • SharePoint for files. No central company database tying it together.

The sharpest pain is reporting. Your financial controller and bookkeeper each spend roughly a week a month pulling data from Xero and Simpro to produce the P&L and operations reports, and a monthly subcontractor spends about a week turning Simpro data into labour forecasts and a board report. The information exists, but it lives in too many places to answer a simple question quickly.

The path

Three phases, each a standalone win

1
The foundation

Consolidate the data & automate reporting

We build pipes from Simpro and Xero (via their APIs) into your first central company database — projects, people, tools, materials, financials, all in one clean place. In parallel, we rebuild the reports your team produces by hand today, so a board pack or a P&L becomes a question answered in minutes instead of a week of compiling. This is the hardest and most valuable step. On its own it removes the reporting drain.

2
The rebuild

Recreate your workflows off the database

With clean data in place, we rebuild the workflows you actually use — cost capture, scheduling, invoicing — running straight from your own database instead of inside Simpro or in spreadsheets. We replace one component at a time, in the order that gives you the most relief first, so nothing breaks and you stay in control of the pace.

3
The replacement

Retire Simpro

Once the workflows you rely on are running on your own system and proven, we decommission Simpro and its licences. The recurring software fee comes off your books, and what remains is a small hosting cost. From here, your system is yours — tailored exactly how you want it, and cheap to run.
