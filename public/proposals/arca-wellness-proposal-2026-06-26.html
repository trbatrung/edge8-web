3. Why Shopify can't anchor the Vietnam core

This is structural, not a preference. Shopify Payments isn't available to a Vietnam-registered business, and a headless storefront still routes the shopper to Shopify's hosted checkout, which is governed by the store's entity country. A Vietnam entity gets pushed onto local gateways anyway. So you're integrating Vietnamese payments directly with or without Shopify, and Shopify's main reason to exist here falls away.

Here's the provider landscape. No single Vietnamese provider covers everything: pick one main gateway for cards, QR, and bank transfer, then add the wallets your customers actually use.

The takeaway. Payoo is a strong, licensed gateway: cards, QR, bank transfer, and instalments in one integration. It does not include MoMo or ZaloPay, Vietnam's two dominant wallets. Plan a main gateway plus those wallets.

Payoo

Pays with: domestic cards (40+ banks), Visa and Mastercard, VietQR, bank transfer, 0% instalments, pay-at-store. Role: broad single-integration gateway. No MoMo or ZaloPay.

VNPAY Strong default

Pays with: VNPAY-QR (40+ banking apps), domestic and international cards, bank transfer. Role: bank-backed and widely used. A sensible primary gateway.

MoMo Add-on

Pays with: the MoMo wallet (Vietnam's #1), QR, linked cards or bank. Role: separate integration. Leaving it out costs conversions.

ZaloPay Add-on

Pays with: the ZaloPay wallet, QR, linked bank or card. Role: tied to Zalo, big reach with younger buyers. Its own integration.

2C2P

Pays with: international and domestic cards, QR, local wallets, bank transfer, instalments, across the region. Role: a regional option if you later want one provider beyond Vietnam.

VietQR & cash on delivery

What they are: VietQR is the national bank-transfer QR standard most apps read, and most gateways present it. Cash on delivery still builds trust on first orders. Keep both.

Provider methods and fees change, and Payoo doesn't publish a full list. Confirm card networks, the wallet list, settlement currency, and pricing with each provider before committing.

7. The open question: fulfilment

At zero to 50 orders a day, fulfilment is an operations choice, not a build blocker. It's the one open decision. Options and our pick:

Start in-house, one carrier Recommended for v1

Pack orders yourself and integrate one Vietnamese carrier (GHN, GHTK, ViettelPost, J&T, or Ninja Van): create a shipment, pull tracking, write it to your database, email the customer. Cheapest, fine well past 50 a day.

3PL pick-pack-ship Later

Outsource warehousing and fulfilment to a Vietnamese 3PL once packing becomes the bottleneck. Premature at your current volume.

Same-day / local courier

For Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi, add an on-demand courier (Ahamove, Grab) for fast local delivery. Optional, same order flow.

How it plugs in

Fulfilment is a phase-2 integration on the same core. It doesn't gate the storefront, payments, or launch. Go live, add carrier automation right after.

Our pick: launch in-house plus one carrier API, and add a 3PL only when volume justifies it.
